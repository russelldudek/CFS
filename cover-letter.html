Dear Justin,

Thank you for reaching out after reviewing my resume. The Data & AI Enablement Lead opportunity is compelling because the mandate sits exactly where many modernization efforts either become useful or stall: between the data foundation, the AI capability, and the way people actually make decisions and perform work.

My view is straightforward. The client does not merely need a centralized repository or another portfolio of AI tools. It needs a trusted operating reality: agreed definitions, accountable data ownership, explicit human authority, workflow-level adoption, and evidence that the intervention improved the business. That is the argument behind the candidate vision I created for our discussion.

At Vape-Jet, I currently lead an AI-first operating transformation across ERP/Odoo, CRM, Jira, Slack, telemetry, production, quality, customer success, support, purchasing, and engineering issue flow. Through DudeWorth, I have developed AI-readiness and agentic-workflow frameworks that connect value, feasibility, data readiness, governance, human judgment, adoption burden, evaluation, and time-to-value. Earlier at Amazon, I built operating mechanisms, visibility, escalation, and standard work in a 24/7 customer-backward environment supporting approximately five million second-day orders annually. At Compunetics, I led engineering, manufacturing, quality, delivery, and process control in advanced, high-reliability settings.

I also want to address the likely concern directly. I have not held a career enterprise data architect title, and I will not imply unsupported ownership of a production Microsoft Fabric estate. I do bring hands-on SQL, Python, API, analytics, ERP, CRM, and AI-workflow experience. More importantly, I know how to own the business architecture around the platform: what the data means, who owns it, which workflows matter, where human authority remains, how vendors and specialists are directed, how adoption is built, and what evidence earns the right to scale.

The accompanying candidate vision, role-aligned resume, interview brief, first-120-days plan, and Operating Lens Review make that operating approach visible. I would welcome the opportunity to learn more about the confidential client, the platform expectations, and the first workflow leadership most needs this role to clarify.

Thank you again for the invitation to connect.
