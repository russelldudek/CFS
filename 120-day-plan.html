Entry principle

Earn the right to scale.

Start with one decision-critical slice. Make its data, ownership, authority, workflow, and evidence visible. Then compound the pattern.

Working assumptions
The end client, systems landscape, operating model, and regulatory context are confidential. This plan is intentionally adaptable and begins with discovery rather than imposing a preselected architecture.
Days 1-30 · Listen and map

Establish operating reality

  • Meet executive sponsors, business leaders, data/IT/security/legal, users, and key vendors.
  • Inventory reporting, CRM, document, workflow, integration, analytics, and AI-tool landscape.
  • Map definitions, ownership, access, quality, lineage, current workarounds, and known risks.
  • Observe high-friction work directly; establish baseline measures and decision consequences.
  • Select one bounded workflow with value, sponsor ownership, usable data, and learnable risk.
Outputs: system and stakeholder map; data/AI risk register; opportunity portfolio; first-workflow charter.
Days 31-60 · Align and bound

Create a trusted slice

  • Name data owners and reconcile canonical definitions for the selected workflow.
  • Define source, transformation, quality thresholds, lineage, access, retention, and exceptions.
  • Clarify target architecture and vendor responsibilities without overbuilding the first use case.
  • Define AI role, prohibited actions, human decision rights, review points, and escalation.
  • Agree outcome, adoption, risk, and learning measures before implementation.
Outputs: trusted data slice; authority/guardrail contract; architecture decision; pilot scorecard.

Day-30 Decision Gate

Proceed only when the first workflow has an accountable business owner, a decision or outcome worth improving, accessible data with visible gaps, an acceptable risk boundary, and enough user participation to learn from real work.

Days 61-90 · Embed and prove

Put one intervention into real work

  • Build or coordinate the smallest useful AI-assisted intervention using the trusted data slice.
  • Integrate at the actual workflow decision point rather than creating a separate demo destination.
  • Train users on both the new action and the reason for human review or escalation.
  • Instrument adoption, workflow outcome, data quality, exception behavior, and user feedback.
  • Run short learning cycles; correct the workflow, data, prompt, control, or ownership as evidence requires.
Outputs: production-bounded pilot; user standard work; support/escalation path; evidence readout.
Days 91-120 · Standardize and scale

Turn one win into capability

  • Decide scale, revise, hold, or retire based on evidence - not sponsorship enthusiasm.
  • Package reusable definitions, connectors, controls, training, evaluation, and decision records.
  • Establish AI tool standards, approved patterns, exception paths, and portfolio governance.
  • Create vendor scorecard and knowledge-transfer requirements; identify internal capability gaps.
  • Sequence the next 2-3 workflow slices by value, readiness, ownership, risk, and reuse.
Outputs: reusable enablement package; portfolio roadmap; vendor operating model; executive scorecard.

Explicit Non-Goals

Do not
Launch a broad AI tool campaign before identifying workflow ownership and evidence.
Do not
Build an enterprise data model detached from the decisions and work it must improve.
Do not
Let vendors become the only people who understand the architecture or operating logic.
Do not
Count access, licenses, demos, or training attendance as value realization.

Decisions to Clarify During Discovery

  1. Expected balance of hands-on platform engineering versus architecture, vendor, and business ownership.
  2. Current cloud, data warehouse/lake, BI, CRM, document, identity, and AI-tool environment.
  3. Regulated data classes, privacy obligations, model-risk requirements, and acceptable-use boundaries.
  4. Executive sponsor, data/IT reporting relationships, and decision rights for standards and funding.
  5. Known data-domain conflicts and the first workflow leadership considers strategically important.
  6. Available internal data engineering, security, architecture, change, and training capacity.

First Leadership Conversation

Ask
Which workflow or data domain currently creates the most consequential uncertainty, rework, risk, or delay?
Clarify
What must this leader personally build, what should internal teams own, and where should vendors accelerate?

Day-120 Executive Readout

What leadership should be able to see

One workflow is trusted, bounded, adopted, measured - and has left a reusable operating pattern behind.

The next portfolio decision is grounded in evidence, not tool excitement.
